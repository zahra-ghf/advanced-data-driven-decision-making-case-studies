# Brand Equity Drivers - Structural Equation Modelling

Business analytics case study - Advanced Data-Driven Decision Making,
GSEM, University of Geneva (Prof. Marcel Paulssen)

## Project Overview

This case study analyzes the key drivers of brand equity for Galeries Lafayette Berlin using exploratory factor analysis, confirmatory factor analysis, and structural equation modelling.

The objective is to identify the main customer perception dimensions and test how these dimensions influence customer satisfaction, affective commitment, repurchase intention, and co-creation intention.

## Business Context

Galeries Lafayette faced declining sales and needed a clearer understanding of how customers perceive the brand and which factors drive loyalty-related outcomes.

The case focuses on understanding how customer image perceptions influence satisfaction, commitment, and behavioral intentions.

## Dataset

The analysis is based on survey data from Galeries Lafayette customers.

The dataset includes:

* Customer perception items related to brand image
* Satisfaction measures
* Affective commitment measures
* Repurchase intention measures
* Co-creation intention measures

The image perception items were measured using Likert-scale survey responses.

## Analytical Approach

The project combines exploratory and confirmatory methods to develop and test a brand equity model.

The analysis includes:

1. Data cleaning
2. Missing value treatment
3. Exploratory Factor Analysis
4. Factorability assessment
5. KMO test
6. Bartlett’s test
7. Factor solution comparison
8. Confirmatory Factor Analysis
9. Structural Equation Modelling
10. Path analysis
11. Interpretation of direct and indirect effects
12. Business recommendations

## Key Methods

* Exploratory Factor Analysis
* Confirmatory Factor Analysis
* Structural Equation Modelling
* Path analysis
* KMO measure of sampling adequacy
* Bartlett’s test
* Factor rotation
* Model fit assessment
* Mediation analysis

## Main Findings

The analysis supports a multidimensional view of brand equity.

Customer perception dimensions were used to explain satisfaction and affective commitment, which in turn were linked to behavioral outcomes such as repurchase intention and co-creation intention.

The structural equation model provides a decision-support framework for understanding which customer perception dimensions are most relevant for brand equity management.
